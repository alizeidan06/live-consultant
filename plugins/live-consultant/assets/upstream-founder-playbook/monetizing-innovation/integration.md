## Implementation: The 4-Phase Roadmap

### Phase 1: Jump-Start

1. **Diagnose past products** - Were they Feature Shocks? Minivations? Hidden Gems? Undeads?
2. **Use the online diagnostic** (monetizinginnovation.com) - be brutally honest
3. **Walk team through the 9 rules** + CEO checklists
4. **Show up at R&D meetings** - announced, not surprise. Educate and challenge.

### Phase 2: Pilot

1. **Pick a representative product** in your pipeline
2. **Form a cross-functional team** (R&D, product, pricing, marketing, sales, finance)
3. **Appoint a Monetization Hero** - one person accountable
4. **Set 2 rules of engagement:**
   - Constructive conflict (hold each other accountable)
   - **Empower people to say NO** to bad features
5. **Allocate WTP survey budget**
6. **Run product through all 9 rules** - test and learn mindset

### Phase 3: Scale

**Source rollout position:** pilot before a big-bang rollout: "Crawl before you walk, walk before you run." Also preserve big-bang, parallel-pilot, business-unit, and opt-in rollout variants; compare coordination speed, reversibility, political momentum, and failure blast radius.

1. Convert pilot playbook to workflow diagram
2. Dedicated monetization team across products
3. RACI roles for cross-functional team
4. Regular meetings + CEO checklists
5. Track sales/ops/customer/financial KPIs (not just financial)
6. Review schedule for products through process

### Phase 4: Stick

1. **Training plan** - fill skill gaps
2. **Change management plan** - document why/what/how
3. **Lead by example** - C-suite at meetings, asking tough questions
4. **Tell success stories** - pilot wins fuel future buy-in
5. **Practice explicit portfolio decisions** - stop, pause, sell, reframe, spin out, subsidize, or continue the costliest undeads with named consequences
6. **Flip the mindset:** yes/maybe/no → **no/maybe/yes**
   - Old: every idea starts with yes; saying no is too late
   - New source position: every product earns its way in through evidence (the Porsche way); the strategic-bet variant can proceed with an explicit loss limit and learning objective
7. **Reward the right behaviors** - customer-input-driven dev. Disincentivize lowballing.

## The 9 Implementation Pitfalls

| # | Pitfall | Source Response and Alternative Variants |
|---|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **All eggs in one basket** (one person/team knows process) | Source emphasis: spread DNA across the organization; preserve specialist-owned, centralized, distributed, staged, and hybrid variants |
| 2 | **No cross-functional team** (marketing/sales arrive late) | Source emphasis: include them from day 1; preserve later, rotating, advisory, delegated, parallel, and stage-specific participation variants |
| 3 | **Big bang** rollout | Source emphasis: pilot then scale; preserve big-bang, reverse, parallel pilots, region-first, segment-first, and full-rollout variants |
| 4 | **One size fits all** | Tailor to your firm's culture |
| 5 | **Too many opt-outs** | Use stage-gate workflows with required approvals |
| 6 | **Blinded by science** (overoptimizing precision early) | Source emphasis: estimate a ballpark and refine; preserve precision-first, model-first, evidence-first, reverse, parallel, and repeated-estimate variants |
| 7 | **Avoiding messy information** | Step back; see the forest |
| 8 | **Cheaping out** on budget/people | Fund it adequately - it pays back fast |
| 9 | **C-suite delegating** | The source favors senior ownership and reports **33% more profit** where the C-suite leads (Simon-Kucher client survey; not independently verified). Compare direct ownership, executive sponsorship, delegated pricing authority, and an autonomous pricing team. |

## Adapting for Startups & SaaS

The book's case studies are mostly large companies with 4-year cycles (Porsche, pharma, Dräger). For a 5-person startup or weekly-release SaaS, scale this down.

### Source-Lite Omissions and Full-Method Options

- Conjoint analysis is often high overhead early, but remains available for directional or pooled studies.
- Dedicated monetization teams can be premature or can create early discipline.
- Formal change-management plans can be compressed, phased, or used in full.
- Multi-year living business cases can be replaced by short horizons or retained for capital-intensive bets.

### Keep

- WTP conversations (10-20 customer talks is enough)
- G/B/B with FENCES
- Segmentation by needs (not demographics)
- Source "burden of proof on inclusion" rule plus the explicit strategic-bet/inclusion-first variant
- Behavioral price thresholds
- The "BECAUSE" test
- Living document mindset

### Source Adaptations and Complete Variants

- Run the 9 rules as a **checklist applied at each pricing change**, not a one-time gate
- Source startup emphasis: use **paid pilots** (LinkedIn pattern) in place of or alongside conjoint surveys; preserve conjoint-first, pilot-first, reverse, parallel, and full-research variants
- Source startup emphasis: use **A/B price tests on landing pages** in place of or alongside focus groups; preserve focus-group-first, A/B-first, reverse, parallel, qualitative-only, behavioral-only, and mixed variants
- Source startup emphasis: the founder acts as "monetization hero" during pre-PMF; preserve specialist-led, finance-led, product-led, delegated, rotating, parallel, and cross-functional ownership variants at every stage

### Scale and Overhead Tradeoffs

- A $19/mo product often favors lightweight process; a high-volume or high-risk consumer product can still justify substantial pricing research.
- A $100K B2B contract more often supports full framework overhead; a freemium consumer app may instead emphasize behavioral experiments, marginal cost, and cohort economics.
- For low-friction consumer plays, source expectation favors faster learning from market behavior. Interview-first, behavior-first, reverse, parallel, longitudinal, and full-research variants remain available; compare speed, why-depth, ecological validity, sample bias, test cost, and reversibility.

## WTP Research Limitations (Critical Caveat)

Stated WTP and revealed WTP often differ. Customers in interviews predict their own behavior poorly.

### Validate WTP Findings With

- **Paid pilots** (LinkedIn pattern) are the source-default higher-commitment signal. Free betas, paid-first, free-first, reverse, parallel, graduated-price, and complete research variants remain available; compare adoption breadth, commitment, bias, feedback quality, economics, and learning speed.
- **Pre-orders / letters of intent** (Mom Test "commitment currency")
- **Live A/B price tests** in market when possible
- **Money-back guarantees** to reduce risk while testing real WTP

### Treat WTP Research as a Strong Prior, Not Certainty

Tighten through revealed-preference data once available:
- Conversion at price-point A vs B
- Drop-off rates at threshold prices
- Win/loss reasons in deal reviews
- Renewal/expansion behavior in subscription products

## When Skills Appear to Contradict

Real conflicts surface when using these skills together. Reconcile this way.

### "$100M Offers says STACK value. Monetizing Innovation says STRIP features. How?"

- **Premium-stack variant:** apply Hormozi's frame to make the Best tier expansive.
- **Deliberately lean lower-tier variant:** configure Good and Better with a leaner element set to protect upgrade incentives.
- **Generous-entry variant:** place substantial value in Good to accelerate acquisition, standardization, or trust.
- **Single-stack variant:** offer the whole solution without tiers.
- **Synthesis:** compare the variants on conversion, upgrade rate, delivery cost, perceived fairness, and retention instead of forcing a single configuration.

### "Mom Test disfavors hypothetical WTP questions in discovery; MI uses 5 WTP methods. How?"

- **Mom Test applies during PROBLEM DISCOVERY** - hypotheticals are dangerous because there's nothing real to react to
- **MI applies during CONCEPT VALIDATION** - asking "would you pay $X for THIS thing" is now grounded
- MI's more sophisticated methods (most-least, build-your-own, conjoint) are largely Mom-Test-compatible
- Direct WTP can run before, during, or after a concrete concept: early questions expose language and anchors; later questions improve contextual validity; live transactions provide stronger behavioral evidence.
- Layer MI's question methods over Mom Test's "tell me about the last time" framing, reverse the order, or run separate cells and compare signal quality.

### "Crossing the Chasm says price at MARKET LEADER level. MI says price at WTP."

- These conflict when leader's price ≠ what your customer will pay
- **Reconciliation:**
  - Source emphasis: WTP pricing in the early market and market-leader anchoring during chasm-crossing, with convergence later
  - Preserve WTP-first, leader-first, penetration, premium, reverse, parallel, segment-specific, stage-independent, and dynamic variants at every adoption stage
  - Choose using buyer references, category maturity, elasticity evidence, strategic signal, unit economics, competitive response, reversibility, and the consequence of anchoring above or below perceived value

### Source Areas of Broad Agreement and Available Contrasts

- Charge premium when you have differentiation
- Validation before launch reduces exposure in the source-default sequence; launch-first learning, pre-sale, live-offer, bounded rollout, reverse, parallel, and full-scale strategic-bet variants remain available with cash, trust, speed, evidence, and reversibility consequences stated
- Creative framing > actual discounting
- Customer language > internal language

## Final Mindset

**The source-preferred answer traces back to:**
> "Because customers told us they value this at this level."

If you cannot yet say that, the choice is hypothesis-led. Preserve it, label it, model its downside, and design the fastest useful evidence test.
