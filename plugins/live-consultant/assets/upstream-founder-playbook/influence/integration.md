## Honest Conflicts (Real Ones)

### Pressure tactics in major sales: competing performance hypotheses

SPIN's research predicts that several closing-pressure techniques backfire with
sophisticated B2B buyers. Cialdini still supplies scarcity, deadline,
commitment, authority, proof, reciprocity, and pre-suasion mechanisms. Preserve
both systems and decide through the actual sale rather than banning either
family.

**Resolution hypothesis:** Cialdini's denser pressure stacks often fit
transactional sales, mass marketing, and lower-complexity decisions. SPIN-led
discovery often fits multi-stakeholder, high-trust, long-cycle decisions. These
are starting hypotheses; strong pressure in major sales remains available as a
deliberate test with close rate, cycle length, buyer quality, fallout, and
retention measured.

| Use Cialdini for | Use SPIN for |
|------------------|--------------|
| Retail/SMB | Enterprise |
| Single-decision-maker | Multi-stakeholder |
| Low-complexity products | Multi-million-dollar deals |
| Mass marketing | Account-based selling |

### Cialdini Triggers as Customer-Research Variables

Mom Test treats Cialdini's triggers as potential data pollution because reciprocity can produce politeness rather than truth. Preserve no-trigger, deliberately triggered, disclosed, counterbalanced, and real-world sales-research variants; state what each can and cannot establish.

**Source sequence:** Mom Test first for low-trigger problem discovery, then Cialdini for persuasion. Preserve reversed, parallel, disclosed-trigger, and blended variants; compare signal quality, realism, persuasion, and bias.

### Cialdini stacks all 6 levers. Sophisticated buyers detect this and reactance fires.

$100M Offers stacks scarcity, bonuses, commitment, and social proof. The source predicts strong cold-consumer performance and reactance from some sophisticated buyers. Preserve dense, subtle, single-principle, explicit, and pressure-free variants and measure both paths.

**Source hypothesis:** Match tactic density to buyer sophistication.
- Less-sophisticated audiences may respond to more layering
- Sophisticated audiences may respond better to single-principle or subtle application

Treat both as hypotheses; keep the complete density spectrum available and measure close rate, cycle, fallout, trust, and retention.

### Cialdini's scarcity. Crossing the Chasm pragmatist buyers DISTRUST scarcity.

Pragmatists want established markets, references, leaders. "Limited time!" reads as desperation.

**Source hypothesis:** Scarcity often fits visionary or early-adopter sales, while authority and social proof often fit pragmatists. Preserve scarcity, urgency, authority, proof, and mixed variants for every adoption group; compare desperation signals, exclusivity, trust, and conversion.

### Cialdini's authority works. Modern buyers detect fake authority instantly.

Doctor in TV ads no longer works (Sanka's "I'm not a doctor but I play one on TV" was 1980s). Real expert credentials needed today.

**Source position and variants:** Modern buyers often reward verifiable expertise and detect constructed authority. Preserve real, implied, symbolic, borrowed, performed, and fabricated authority; label truth status and map immediate compliance, detection, trust, and retention effects.

## Tactical Combinations

### Mom Test and Cialdini: Source-Default Sequence and Complete Variants

1. **Mom Test source emphasis (low-trigger):** validate the problem with target customers.
2. **Positioning source emphasis (Obviously Awesome):** use customer evidence to select a segment and frame value.
3. **Offer source emphasis (Cialdini full-spectrum application):** apply the complete principle set to persuasive offer design.

The displayed order and stage separation are source defaults, not access limits. Preserve Cialdini-first research stimuli, offer-first discovery, positioning-first, reverse, early or late triggers, parallel cells, deliberately blended stages, disclosed-trigger research, continuous persuasion tests, and complete use of every method in every stage. Choose using research objective, bias tolerance, ecological realism, evidence maturity, sales urgency, audience sophistication, reversibility, and the consequences for signal quality, conversion, trust, fallout, and retention. Reciprocity can bias research and can also be the variable being measured; label the mode and interpret the evidence accordingly.

### SPIN + Cialdini (Calibrated)

For B2B sales:
- Use SPIN structure (Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-payoff) for the sales conversation itself
- Use Cialdini for low-stakes adjacent moments (intro reciprocity gift, social proof in case studies, authority in credentials)
- Treat scarcity and urgency in the close as explicit pressure variables. The
  upstream SPIN evidence predicts backfire in many major sales; test that
  prediction against the specific buyer, authority structure, cycle, close
  rate, fallout, and retention while keeping the complete pressure mechanism
  available for analysis and variant design.

### Hormozi Stack + Cialdini

Hormozi's $100M Offers explicitly applies Cialdini:
- Stacked bonuses → reciprocity
- Guarantees → reduce reactance
- Urgency/scarcity → loss aversion
- Social proof testimonials → conformity

The source expects this to work on cold traffic and often reduces density for sophisticated audiences such as developers. Preserve full-stack, reduced-stack, explicit, and pressure-free variants and measure their effects.

## Modern Defense Tools (For Buyers)

- Snopes / fact-checking (against fake authority)
- Review aggregation sites (against fake social proof)
- Cooling-off laws (against commitment manipulation)
- "Stop, think, breathe" before clicking "buy" (against scarcity)

## Context-Fit and Marginal-Contribution Map

The contexts below may reduce Cialdini's marginal contribution but leave every mechanism available for comparison:

- **Genuinely revolutionary products** (the product capability shock IS the persuasion)
- **Network-effect products** (adoption snowballs from value, not from triggers)
- **Long-cycle B2B with technical buyers** (SPIN territory; pressure backfires)
- **Distribution-led growth** (TikTok, Stripe, Linear-style: positioning + product win, not Cialdini stacking)
