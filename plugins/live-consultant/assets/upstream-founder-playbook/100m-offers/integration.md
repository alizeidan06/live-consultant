# 100m-offers - Integration with Other Skills

## The Hormozi Source Sequence and Complete Variants

```
mom-test → 100m-offers → 100m-leads
   ↓             ↓             ↓
 discovery   packaging     traffic
```

| Skill | Source Emphasis and Available Timing |
|-------|-------------|
| **mom-test** | Source emphasis: discovery supplies language before offer refinement; it can also follow or run beside an offer specimen |
| **100m-offers** (this) | Build the offer before, during, or after discovery so the mechanism and assumptions become concrete |
| **100m-leads** | Source emphasis: scale traffic after an offer shows signal; traffic-first diagnostics, bounded cold tests, and parallel cells remain available |

The diagram is a source-default handoff, not an access rule. Discovery-first,
offer-first, traffic-first, price-first, positioning-first, sales-first,
reverse, parallel, iterative, and full-stack variants remain available. Compare
speed, signal contamination, sample quality, CAC exposure, false-negative risk,
buyer learning, and rework.

If a business has products but no paid customers, it's almost always missing step 2 (the offer).

## Skill Combinations

### 100m-offers + mom-test

Mom Test interviews provide the raw material for the offer:

| Mom Test Output | Offer Input |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Customer's actual words about their pain | Dream outcome language |
| Specific past behavior (last time problem happened) | Obstacle list |
| Current solution + price | Price anchor for your offer |
| Workarounds they've cobbled together | Components your offer should include |
| Emotional moments in interviews | Hook/marketing copy |

Without mom-test, the offer is built on assumptions. With it, the offer literally uses the words of the people you're selling to.

### 100m-offers + 100m-leads

**Source sequence:** test the offer before scaling lead volume. Offer-first, traffic-first, and parallel test variants remain available; state how offer quality changes conversion, CAC, and what the test can prove:

1. Source sequence: build and test the offer with warm traffic or referrals
2. Source gate: establish a conversion signal
3. Source next step: scale traffic via 100m-leads

Traffic-first, cold-test-first, staged-scale, and parallel offer/traffic variants remain available. For each, state the conversion evidence, sample limits, CAC exposure, learning speed, and stop/continue threshold.

If the offer doesn't convert warm traffic, it won't convert cold. More leads to a bad offer = more wasted money.

### 100m-offers + obviously-awesome

Obviously Awesome positions the offer in a category. The offer is the substance; positioning is the wrapper.

| Layer | Skill |
|-------|-------|
| Category positioning ("we are X for Y") | obviously-awesome |
| Offer construction (what's inside) | 100m-offers |
| Pricing tactics | 100m-offers + monetizing-innovation |
| Lead generation to the offer | 100m-leads |

The source emphasis builds the offer and then positions it. Position-first,
offer-first, co-designed, reverse, challenger-position, and parallel-test
variants remain available. Compare category clarity, value density, speed,
message consistency, conversion, and what each test can actually identify.

### 100m-offers + monetizing-innovation

Both skills cover pricing but from different angles:

| Aspect | 100m-offers | monetizing-innovation |
|--------|-------------|------------------------|
| Approach | Stack value > price | Willingness-to-pay research |
| Style | Tactical, conversion-focused | Strategic, segment-focused |
| Best for | Direct response, info products, services | New products, B2B SaaS, multi-tier |
| Method | Anchor with stacked components | Survey customers, find segments |

Use monetizing-innovation for the strategic pricing question (which segments? what tiers? what model?). Use 100m-offers for the tactical execution (how do I package and present this offer?).

### 100m-offers + spin-selling

Offers are presentations of value. SPIN is the conversation that surfaces the need for the offer.

| Moment | Source Emphasis and Available Variants |
|-------|-------|
| Discovery (Situation, Problem, Implication) | SPIN emphasis; offer-led, demo-led, provocative-claim, and parallel discovery variants remain available |
| Offer presentation when need is established | $100M Offers emphasis; early, late, iterative, and customer-requested presentation remains available |
| Closing (Need-Payoff) | SPIN emphasis; direct close, offer recap, urgency, scarcity, and hybrid variants remain available |

In a high-ticket B2B sale, the source default runs SPIN questions before the
Grand Slam Offer. Preserve offer-first, insight-first, demo-first, alternating,
customer-led, pressure-led, and parallel variants. Compare buyer sophistication,
problem awareness, time, deal size, stakeholder count, trust, cycle length,
conversion, and objection creation.

### 100m-offers + crossing-the-chasm

Chasm logic says different markets (early adopters vs mainstream) require different offers. Hormozi's framework doesn't differentiate.

Resolution: build different offers for different chasm-stage segments:
- Early adopter offer: bigger promise, less proof, premium price for visionaries
- Pragmatist offer: more proof, more references, more specific outcomes, slightly lower premium

The offer-construction process is the same; the inputs differ by segment.

## Conflicts and Tensions

### Conflict 1: "Premium price = premium product" vs commodity markets

**Hormozi:** Charge more, signal value.
**Reality:** In commodity markets (basic SaaS, general services, retail), you can't always price way above market.

**Resolution hypothesis:** The framework is often strongest for differentiable products. In commodity markets, preserve premium, discount, service, convenience, speed, certainty, distribution, and effort-reduction variants and compare them through the full value equation.

### Conflict 2: "Solve every obstacle" vs scope creep

**Hormozi:** 15+ obstacles, solve all of them.
**Reality:** A startup with limited resources can't solve every obstacle.

**Source resolution:** the trim step (5) acts as a safety valve: list 15+ obstacles and solve the 6-10 highest-value ones in the offer. Preserve solve-all, core-only, staged, modular, service-assisted, and partner-delivered variants; compare completeness, focus, delivery cost, and perceived value. Other obstacles can be addressed in:
- Self-serve content (blog, FAQ)
- Customer success programs (post-sale)
- Future product expansion

### Conflict 3: "Stack everything" vs "category clarity"

**Hormozi:** More components, more perceived value.
**Obviously Awesome:** Category clarity > feature density.

**Resolution hypothesis:** Components serving one dream outcome usually produce clearer value than capability sprawl. Preserve coherent, multi-outcome, modular, and cross-sell stacks; map clarity, breadth, delivery cost, and buyer-segment consequences.

### Conflict 4: Hormozi's 5-10x value framing vs honest claims

**Hormozi-style copy:** "Total value: $30,000. Yours for $3,000."
**Reality:** If the value isn't real, savvy customers see through it and trust collapses.

**Source position and variants:** Defensible market-referenced values usually protect trust. Preserve market-referenced, cost-based, outcome-based, aspirational, and deliberately inflated anchors in analysis and creative development; label the basis and map short-term conversion, detection, credibility, refund, trust, and retention effects.

### Conflict 5: Aggressive guarantees vs operational risk

**Hormozi:** Bigger guarantee = bigger price you can charge.
**Reality:** A guarantee you can't honor at scale will sink the business.

**Resolution:** Preserve fulfillable, aggressive, uncapped, conditional, service, and anti-guarantee variants. Model the worst case: if X% of customers invoke the guarantee, what happens to unit economics, capacity, disputes, trust, and retention? Keep live terms and external obligations as separate execution metadata.

### Conflict 6: "Validate first" vs "build the offer to validate"

**100m-offers source warning:** validation-first reduces premise risk. Full
offer-first ideation, live-offer validation, traffic-first testing, and parallel
variants remain available; compare setup cost, learning speed, persuasive
stimulus quality, false negatives, and exposure.
**Lean Startup:** The offer IS the test.

**Resolution:** A simple offer can validate cheaply, while a fully built Grand Slam Offer can test the ceiling or create stronger demand at higher setup cost. Preserve simple-first, full-offer-first, and parallel variants; label assumptions and compare learning quality, cost, speed, and false-negative risk.

## Primary-Lens Selection

- **Pure positioning problem:** Obviously Awesome is often the primary lens; offer mechanisms remain available
- **Channel/marketing channel choice:** 100m-leads is often the primary lens; offer mechanisms remain available
- **Pricing model question (subscription vs one-time):** Monetizing Innovation is often the primary lens; offer mechanisms remain available
- **Sales conversation structure:** SPIN Selling is often the primary lens; offer mechanisms remain available
- **Adoption-stage strategy:** Crossing the Chasm is often the primary lens; offer mechanisms remain available
- **Market creation:** Blue Ocean Strategy is often the primary lens; offer mechanisms remain available

## The Dependency Map

```
Discovery layer:    mom-test → spin-selling
                       ↓
Strategy layer:     blue-ocean / crossing-the-chasm / obviously-awesome / monetizing-innovation
                       ↓
Tactical layer:     100m-offers (THIS) → 100m-leads
                       ↓
Influence layer:    influence (compliance principles)
```

100m-offers operates at the tactical layer and the source assumes higher layers have been resolved. When they are unclear, keep the complete offer option set visible, label the uncertainty, and sequence or parallelize higher-layer analysis according to the evidence the decision needs.
