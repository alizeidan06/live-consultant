# Build Business Operations

At the start of every invocation, read and apply the mandatory
[Live Consultant communication voice](../founder-business-consultant/references/communication-voice.md).
Use it for every user-facing answer and customer-facing artifact. Simplify the
language, never the reasoning, stakes, specificity, or commercial force.

Before selecting research, examples, or tactics, also read and apply the
[niche intelligence protocol](../founder-business-consultant/references/niche-intelligence-protocol.md).
Infer the niche from available context first. Ask only questions whose answers
could change the research route or recommendation; otherwise proceed with a
labeled reversible assumption.

Build only what the current proof and stage require. A business operating system
must survive when no agent is running.

## Confirm the build is authorized

Before implementation, verify:

- the offer and buyer state;
- the approved scope, jurisdiction, budget, and decision owner;
- what may be drafted versus published, connected, purchased, or changed;
- dependencies requiring credentials, professional review, contracts, payment
  processing, customer data, or external communication.

Do not treat entity, tax, employment, insurance, contract, privacy, or regulated
advice as universal. Use [business-setup-checklist.md](references/business-setup-checklist.md)
to create questions and evidence for qualified local review.

## Design from the customer promise backward

1. Define the accepted customer, scope, exclusions, deliverable, time-to-value,
   revisions, support, refund/remedy, and completion evidence.
2. Map intake → qualification → payment → onboarding → production → QA →
   delivery → support → retention/referral → accounting close.
3. Assign one owner, one input, one output, one service level, and one exception
   path to each step.
4. Model capacity and failure modes before acquisition volume.
5. Add the minimum systems that reduce real risk or recurring cost; avoid an
   automation stack without a defined process.

## Establish controls

- Separate measured values from forecasts.
- Reconcile revenue, refunds, fees, COGS, delivery labor, support, commissions,
  taxes, ad spend, cash timing, and liabilities.
- Use processor-hosted payment flows and least-privilege account access.
- Collect the minimum customer data, state its purpose, protect it, and define
  retention/deletion.
- Document claims, guarantees, scarcity, testimonials, licenses, consent, and
  approvals.
- Define backup, incident, complaint, refund, chargeback, and access-loss paths.

## Build the minimum durable stack

Create or reuse:

- business context and decision log;
- offer/delivery specification and QA checklist;
- customer intake and support runbook;
- unit-economics and cash model;
- website/funnel requirements and event map;
- CRM stages, source-of-truth fields, and ownership;
- experiment register and learning ledger;
- launch gates, rollback, and post-launch review.

Use `../../scripts/init_founder_workspace.py` and templates under
`../../assets/templates/` when the user lacks an existing structure.

## Verify end to end

Test the actual journey on mobile and desktop, event recording, payment sandbox,
confirmation, delivery, access permissions, support response, refund path, and
financial reconciliation. Report what was mechanically tested, what still
needs a human/professional check, and what remains approval-bound.
