# Logic protocol

## Claim test

For every material claim ask:

1. What exactly is being claimed?
2. What would have to be true for it to hold?
3. What observation supports each premise?
4. Could the same evidence arise if the claim were false?
5. What scope, population, time, channel, and measurement does it cover?
6. What evidence would change the conclusion?

## Common business reasoning failures

- Interest, search, or compliments treated as willingness to pay.
- Competitor presence treated as disqualification or automatic opportunity.
- Revenue, ROAS, or dashboard attribution treated as contribution profit.
- Post-hoc change treated as causal lift without a counterfactual.
- Selected wins generalized while failures and denominators disappear.
- A framework applied outside its stage, buyer, sale, or channel.
- A precise forecast built from unmeasured inputs.
- Sunk cost, status quo, novelty, authority, or action bias deciding silently.
- Several correlated signals counted as independent proof.
- A reversible test framed as an irreversible strategy choice.

## Causal chain

Write the proposed mechanism:

```text
action → exposure → buyer interpretation → behavior → delivery → retention → cash
```

Attach evidence and failure modes to every arrow. If one link is unsupported,
the outcome claim remains a hypothesis.

## Counterframework rule

For important decisions, state the strongest framework that recommends a
different action. Compare their hidden assumptions; do not blend conclusions.
