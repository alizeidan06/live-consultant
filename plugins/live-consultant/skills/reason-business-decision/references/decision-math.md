# Decision math

## Expected value

For mutually exclusive outcomes:

```text
EV = Σ(probability × value) - certain costs
```

Use ranges and dependencies. Probabilities must sum to one within each scenario
set. Include cash timing, survival risk, and the value of learning or preserved
options where material.

## Break-even

Solve for the uncertain input that makes the decision neutral. Useful examples:

```text
break-even conversion rate = required customers / qualified visitors
maximum CAC = risk-adjusted contribution - required profit reserve
break-even CPC = maximum CAC × click-to-customer rate
break-even CPL = maximum CAC × lead-to-customer rate
break-even volume = fixed incremental cost / contribution per sale
```

## Sensitivity

Vary one important assumption at a time, then test correlated downside. Rank
inputs by how much they change the verdict. Do not spend research effort on an
input that cannot change the decision.

## Decision quality versus outcome quality

A good decision can have a bad realized outcome. Judge the process using the
information available at the time, then update probabilities from the result.
Never rewrite the original assumptions after the fact.
