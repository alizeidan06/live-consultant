# King Kong paid-acquisition source synthesis

Sources reviewed:

- `Meta-Ads-Playbook.pdf`, 73 PDF pages.
- `5waystodoublesales (1).pdf`, 24 PDF pages; recovered with visual OCR because
  its embedded character map was corrupt.

Both are agency lead-generation assets and end in agency calls to action. Use
them as heuristics, not independent performance evidence.

## Durable or useful with stronger gates

- Buyer-pain/Halo research from search behavior, forums, groups, and reviews
  (`5 Ways`, pp.4–8). This nominates problems; require exact-buyer behavior,
  spend/effort, consequence, price, and counterevidence.
- Prove one channel's contribution economics before adding channels for
  resilience (`5 Ways`, pp.10–11).
- Use awareness states as message labels; discard the universal 3/17/20/60
  percentages (`5 Ways`, pp.12–13; Meta p.10).
- A useful lead magnet solves one real micro-problem, attracts the intended
  buyer, gains valid consent, and bridges naturally (`5 Ways`, pp.13–17).
- Strong, relevant headline, clear offer, and one action (`5 Ways`, pp.18–20).
- Entry offers are optional economics tests, not default $7–$10 products
  (`5 Ways`, pp.21–23).
- Reframe the source's “hyperdopamine” creative as
  `attention → relevance → substantiated benefit → proof → action` (Meta
  pp.13–31).
- Test lead-ins, length, CTAs, formats, and variants rather than treating them as
  universal winners (Meta pp.32–41).
- Preserve audience/problem/promise/proof/action through the landing page (Meta
  pp.42–44).
- Place account architecture behind proof, economics, policy, tracking, and
  minimum-data gates (Meta pp.46–64).
- Diagnose before deleting and reconcile business metrics monthly (Meta
  pp.66–70).
- Platform tactics have short half-lives (Meta p.72).

## Reject or quarantine

- “Creative is 80%” as a causal rule. The governing sequence is proof,
  capability, economics, compliance, tracking, creative/page, then structure.
- “Broad beats everything,” fixed ad volumes, universal CTAs, fixed review
  windows, and universal budget ceilings.
- Spend allocation as the definition of a winner (Meta p.48); business
  contribution and incrementality govern.
- “One-day view is a lie” as a substitute for incrementality analysis.
- Universal purchaser exclusion; retention, replenishment, and upsell have
  different objectives.
- “Double sales,” “10× more likely,” and other unsupported outcome claims.
- The “not a sales call” claim in the explicit sales CTA (`5 Ways`, p.24).

## Correct the economics

Meta p.70 compares `$10k × 15 = $150k` revenue with `$100k × 5 = $500k`.
Incremental revenue is `$350k`, but incremental ad spend is `$90k`; maximum
incremental cash before other variable cost is `$260k`, not `$350k`. At a 30%
pre-ad contribution margin, contribution after ads moves from `$35k` to `$50k`,
only `$15k`.

The bid formulas on Meta p.57 use unsubstantiated 1.15 factors. Preserve them
only as a sandbox hypothesis with a user-measured incrementality factor; never
as default operating math.
