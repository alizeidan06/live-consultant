# King Kong paid-acquisition source synthesis

Sources reviewed:

- `Meta-Ads-Playbook.pdf`, 73 PDF pages.
- `5waystodoublesales (1).pdf`, 24 PDF pages; recovered with visual OCR because
  its embedded character map was corrupt.

Both are agency lead-generation assets and end in agency calls to action. Use
them as heuristics, not independent performance evidence.

## Durable or useful with stronger gates

- Buyer-pain/Halo research from search behavior, forums, groups, and reviews
  (`5 Ways`, pp.4–8). This nominates problems; require exact-buyer behavior,
  spend/effort, consequence, price, and counterevidence.
- The source default learns one channel's contribution economics and then adds
  channels for resilience (`5 Ways`, pp.10–11). Parallel portfolios,
  simultaneous discovery, channel-before-offer learning, and resilience-first
  launches remain available; compare attribution clarity, learning speed, cash
  exposure, team capacity, channel interaction, and concentration risk.
- Use awareness states as message labels and preserve the source's universal
  3/17/20/60 percentages as a historical hypothesis to explain and test (`5
  Ways`, pp.12–13; Meta p.10).
- Analyze the complete lead-magnet and contact-acquisition spectrum, including
  opt-in, permission-based, paid-list, scraped-list, third-party-list, and
  unsolicited variants. For recommended or executed external acquisition,
  select among them using current consent, law, platform policy,
  deliverability, buyer response, economics, and owner authorization (`5 Ways`,
  pp.13–17).
- Strong, relevant headline, clear offer, and one action (`5 Ways`, pp.18–20).
- Entry offers are optional economics tests, not default $7–$10 products
  (`5 Ways`, pp.21–23).
- Preserve the source's complete “hyperdopamine” mechanism, rhetoric, intensity
  levers, examples, expected effects, and failure modes. Then distinguish a
  current substantiated-execution variant—`attention → relevance → benefit
  direction with factual status → proof → action`—without replacing the source
  method (Meta pp.13–31).
- Test lead-ins, length, CTAs, formats, and variants rather than treating them as
  universal winners (Meta pp.32–41).
- Preserve audience/problem/promise/proof/action through the landing page (Meta
  pp.42–44).
- Keep every source account-architecture variant available for explanation,
  comparison, and sandbox design. Assign `LAUNCH-READY` to an external
  architecture only after proof, economics, policy, tracking, and minimum-data
  status are established (Meta pp.46–64).
- Diagnose before deleting and reconcile business metrics monthly (Meta
  pp.66–70).
- Platform tactics have short half-lives (Meta p.72).

## Preserve the source methods and test them in context

These are part of the supplied playbooks. Keep them available as creative,
diagnostic, or experimental hypotheses instead of deleting or quarantining
them. Their source wording does not automatically make them a measured fact or
a universal operating rule.

- Treat “creative is 80%” as a forceful prioritization hypothesis. Let it drive
  a strong creative pass, then measure how much variation is actually caused
  by offer, audience, page, tracking, auction, and delivery in this account.
- Treat “broad beats everything,” fixed ad volumes, universal CTAs, fixed
  review windows, and universal budget ceilings as testable starting points.
  Route them by account maturity, conversion volume, audience, objective, and
  cash tolerance.
- Preserve spend allocation as the source's winner signal (Meta p.48), and
  compare it with contribution, incrementality, lead quality, and downstream
  sales at every scale state. Scale-first, bounded-learning, staged, and
  holdout-led variants remain available; those signals weight spend, downside,
  and expected consequences rather than access to the method.
- Use “one-day view is a lie” as a provocative prompt to inspect observation
  windows and delayed conversion. It does not replace incrementality analysis.
- Test purchaser exclusion by objective. Acquisition, retention,
  replenishment, cross-sell, and upsell can require different treatment.
- Keep “double sales,” “10× more likely,” and similar outcome language as
  source-reported or illustrative claim directions until the relevant
  business produces evidence for them.
- Preserve the “not a sales call” specimen from the explicit sales CTA (`5
  Ways`, p.24) as a complete conversion example. Label its source status and
  analyze the mechanism as written; an external description of a real call
  states its actual function accurately, then tests the effect on trust and
  show-up quality.

## Correct the economics

Meta p.70 compares `$10k × 15 = $150k` revenue with `$100k × 5 = $500k`.
Incremental revenue is `$350k`, but incremental ad spend is `$90k`; maximum
incremental cash before other variable cost is `$260k`, not `$350k`. At a 30%
pre-ad contribution margin, contribution after ads moves from `$35k` to `$50k`,
only `$15k`.

The bid formulas on Meta p.57 use unsubstantiated 1.15 factors. Preserve and
explain them as complete source math, and mark them `SANDBOX HYPOTHESIS` until a
user-measured incrementality factor supports a current operating recommendation.
