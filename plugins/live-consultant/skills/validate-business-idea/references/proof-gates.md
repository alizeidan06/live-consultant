# Proof gates

## Gate 0: scope integrity

- One buyer role, one trigger, one problem scope, one price context.
- Sources are independent enough to count separately.
- Every occurrence has a locator, date, access status, classification, and
  rationale. Inaccessible sources are nonproof, not negative evidence.

## Gate 1: problem and consequence

Require current buyer-originated evidence of the problem or job and why it
matters. Search language and comments can nominate a problem but do not prove
material consequence.

## Gate 2: behavior and transaction context

Look for completed spend, repeated manual effort, switching behavior, budget,
deadline, risk, or an existing paid category. Do not require every free
alternative to fail; test why some buyers still pay.

## Gate 3: value and price

Show a concrete specimen or delivery concept. Test a realistic price and a
real tradeoff. A waitlist click, compliment, or hypothetical answer is weaker
than a deposit, purchase, signed pilot, or observed budget allocation.

## Gate 4: capability and delivery

Verify skills, rights, dependencies, quality control, turnaround, revisions,
support, capacity, and failure remedies. Do not promise an outcome the operator
cannot control.

## Gate 5: economics and cash

Model price, discounts, refunds, COGS, payment fees, variable delivery labor,
support, commissions, chargebacks, fully loaded CAC, payback, retention,
capacity, and cash timing under conservative/base/optimistic cases.

## Gate 6: compliance and permission

Verify current jurisdiction, professional requirements, privacy/consent,
claims, IP, platform, processor, tax, age, and data-use constraints. Qualified
review is required when stakes or uncertainty are material.

## Gate discipline

Passing a gate authorizes only the next bounded test. It does not authorize
public launch, spend, payment connection, account changes, or outreach unless
the owner explicitly approves those actions.
