# Validate Business Idea

At the start of every invocation, read and apply the mandatory
[Live Consultant communication voice](../founder-business-consultant/references/communication-voice.md).
Use it for every user-facing answer and customer-facing artifact. Simplify the
language, never the reasoning, stakes, specificity, or commercial force.

Before selecting research, examples, or tactics, also read and apply the
[niche intelligence protocol](../founder-business-consultant/references/niche-intelligence-protocol.md).
Infer the niche from available context first. Ask only questions whose answers
could change the research route or recommendation; otherwise proceed with a
labeled reversible assumption.

Protect the user from building the most buildable idea instead of the most
wanted, deliverable, and economically viable one.

## Freeze the candidate

Record one candidate per lane:

- exact buyer and trigger moment;
- painful job or desired progress in buyer language;
- current alternatives, workarounds, and spend;
- proposed outcome, delivery, scope, time, and price band;
- founder capability, capacity, and unfair advantage;
- channel hypothesis, geography, jurisdiction, and regulated status;
- assumptions that must be true.

Do not merge buyer roles, sources, seller cohorts, or different problem scopes
to manufacture proof.

## Run the proof gates

Read [proof-gates.md](references/proof-gates.md) and use
[evidence-schema.md](references/evidence-schema.md).

1. **Problem:** exact buyers show a current, material problem or desired job.
2. **Behavior:** they spend money, meaningful time, reputation, or operating
   effort on it; alternatives need not have universally failed.
3. **Paid preference:** evidence distinguishes compliments and interest from a
   real tradeoff at a realistic price.
4. **Value gap:** a defined segment remains underserved on outcome, certainty,
   speed, effort, proof, delivery, terms, or risk.
5. **Capability:** the founder can produce a credible specimen and fulfill the
   promise at acceptable quality.
6. **Economics:** contribution, CAC range, payback, cash timing, capacity, and
   downside can work under conservative inputs.
7. **Compliance:** no unresolved legal, privacy, platform, claims, IP, payment,
   or data-use blocker.

Competition is evidence that a category may transact. It is neither a kill
condition nor proof that this segment and offer will win.

## Test in increasing-cost order

Prefer the smallest test that can disprove the most important assumption:

```text
primary-source observation
→ non-leading customer conversation
→ behavior or completed-spend evidence
→ narrow specimen
→ exact-price tradeoff or paid-intent test
→ controlled fulfillment
→ channel test
→ scale test
```

Do not use final copy, a public funnel, paid ads, checkout integration, or a
full product to simulate evidence that earlier gates lack.

## Decide

- `ADVANCE`: all gates needed for the next test have evidence.
- `REVISE`: one bounded reframe changes a substantive variable.
- `PARK`: potentially useful but missing capability, timing, or accessible
  evidence.
- `REJECT`: a critical assumption failed and the reframe repeats the same
  failure.
- `INSUFFICIENT EVIDENCE`: the bounded test ended without enough proof.

Return the strongest disconfirming evidence, explicit unknowns, the one next
test, pass/stop thresholds, and what the result would authorize. An honest
`NONE` is a valid outcome.
