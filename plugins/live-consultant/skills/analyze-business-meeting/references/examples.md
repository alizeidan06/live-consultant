# Business meeting analysis examples

All examples are illustrative.

## Minimized hosted-routing synopsis

```text
Decision: Convert an inventory-heavy distributor meeting into an immediate
cash-release plan and a durable operating plan.
Niche facts: established B2B importer/distributor; several buyer segments;
tactile product; founder dependence.
Issues: unreliable inventory and landed-cost visibility, urgent cash pressure,
verbal demand before purchasing, channel concentration, repeated sales-hiring
failure, and proposed automation before a manual baseline.
Deliverable: evidence-grade meeting brief, inventory economics, 13-week cash
actions, owners, thresholds, and complete skill stack.
```

This is sufficient for routing. It excludes the raw record, identities,
locations, exact private numbers, and quotations.

## Statement ledger

| Normalized statement | Attribution | Evidence status | Claim type | Meeting function | Treatment |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Near-term cash is the priority | HIGH | reported | constraint | decision | objective lock pending metric/date |
| A new segment will buy more | MEDIUM | unsupported | forecast | proposal | require segment economics and commitment test |
| Last month was unprofitable | HIGH | reported | estimate | statement | reconcile finance source before asserting profit |
| Automating the process will fix sales | LOW | unsupported | causal claim | diagnosis | test role, process, data, and lead-quality alternatives |

## Objective-drift record

```text
Opening objective: release cash inside 13 weeks.
Middle emphasis: brand, website, research, and AI infrastructure.
Closing plan: gather broad data and revisit.
Drift: the closing plan has no weekly cash action or survival floor.
Repair: create an immediate cash lane now; keep the brand/data system as a
separate durable lane.
```

## Decision and action ledger

| State | Item | Owner | Due | Metric/next decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| DECIDED | build SKU-age and landed-cost baseline | finance/ops owner | Friday | coverage of active SKUs; decide liquidation and reorder rules |
| PROPOSED | test a new direct segment | unassigned | unknown | deposit-backed commitments; decide whether to expand |
| ASSIGNED | collect open quotes and receivables | sales owner | Wednesday | cash value and due dates; choose follow-up queue |
| DISCUSSED | replace sales work with AI | none | none | no action until process baseline exists |

## Compact final brief

```text
Bottom line: the meeting named a cash emergency but closed on a broad long-term
system. Restore the 13-week cash objective before funding the bigger build.

Established: stock exists, records are incomplete, several channels matter,
and the founder carries key work.
Not established: verified profit, SKU contribution, the best segment, why sales
hires failed, or that automation solves the constraint.

Now: reconcile inventory age, landed contribution, receivables, quotes, and
purchase commitments. Assign one owner and weekly cash metric.
Later: build the data, brand, funnel, sales, and automation system using the
baseline and measured bottleneck.
```
