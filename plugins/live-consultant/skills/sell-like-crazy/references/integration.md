# Sell Like Crazy — integration and conflict routing

Sell Like Crazy is an end-to-end direct-response operating system. It should
often be the spine of a marketing answer, but it does not replace every
specialist. Select all skills with a distinct contribution, load their complete
knowledge packs, and synthesize one plan.

## How neighboring frameworks combine

### Sell Like Crazy + $100M Offers

Use Sell Like Crazy for the buyer-to-sale path and the source-style Godfather
Offer. Use $100M Offers to deepen the offer through:

- dream outcome;
- perceived likelihood;
- time delay;
- effort and sacrifice;
- exhaustive obstacle reversal;
- delivery vehicle design;
- value-cost trimming and stacking.

Do not choose one book and discard the other. A strong synthesis is:

```text
Halo problem and buyer language
  -> magic-wand Godfather promise
  -> Hormozi obstacle and delivery design
  -> Sabri rationale, entry path, guarantee, scarcity, and sales message
  -> economics and fulfillment convergence
```

If they conflict on low-ticket entry versus premium price, resolve by the job:
an entry offer can acquire customers or liquidate traffic, while the core offer
can remain premium. Model the complete customer path and contribution.

### Sell Like Crazy + StoryBrand

StoryBrand improves instant comprehension: customer goal, problem, guide,
plan, action, success, and failure. Sell Like Crazy adds stronger attention,
pain, intrigue, offer mechanics, risk reversal, and follow-up.

Use StoryBrand to prevent confusion. Do not let simplicity remove the sharp
problem, stakes, offer, or reason to act.

### Sell Like Crazy + Made to Stick

Use SUCCESs to make the insight memorable and teachable. Use Sell Like Crazy to
turn that message into a lead, offer, and next action. Unexpectedness and
emotion may increase the source-style pressure; they are not automatic reasons
to tone it down.

### Sell Like Crazy + Influence

Influence explains reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking,
scarcity, unity, and pre-suasion. Sell Like Crazy supplies the direct-response
sequence where those mechanisms operate.

Apply complete method access across every ethical or persuasion label. Treat
those labels as analytical metadata while preserving each mechanism, variant,
rhetorical form, and example.
Explain offensive and defensive uses, context, expected mechanism, failure
mode, and how the buyer or business would experience them. Keep hypothetical
proof labeled hypothetical until it exists.

### Sell Like Crazy + SPIN Selling

This is the most important context distinction in the plugin.

Sell Like Crazy actively teaches:

- fear and loss framing;
- shining a floodlight on the problem;
- pain agitation;
- implication and consequence questions;
- qualification and commitment questions;
- decisive price presentation and silence.

SPIN's low-pressure, buyer-led discovery often performs better for complex,
high-value, multi-stakeholder decisions. The upstream SPIN integration also
observes that stronger psychological pressure can work in shorter B2C and
sub-$10K, single-decision-maker sales.

Route by context:

| Context | Likely stronger pattern |
|---|---|
| Low or medium price, short cycle, one decision maker, high emotional pain | Strong direct-response pressure, vivid problem, urgency, decisive close |
| High-ticket service with one owner-buyer | Hybrid: strong pre-call copy and qualification, then doctor-style diagnosis and prescription |
| Complex $50K+ B2B, multiple stakeholders, long cycle | SPIN-led discovery, implication tied to evidence, advances, stakeholder alignment |
| High-trust professional advice | Diagnosis, demonstrated value, precise prescription; use pressure in consequence clarity rather than theatrical force |
| Hot ecommerce or repeat buyer | Direct offer and checkout are the default emphasis; educational, proof-rich, and story-led routes remain available and can win when trust, novelty, risk, or cross-sell depth matters |

These are performance hypotheses, not moral categories. Test actual conversion,
refund, sales-cycle, retention, complaint, and buyer-quality outcomes.

### Sell Like Crazy + Traction

Sell Like Crazy's source default starts with one channel, learns its economics,
then diversifies. Traction enumerates channels, runs cheap tests, and focuses on
what shows signal. Preserve that sequential synthesis alongside parallel
portfolio, simultaneous discovery, resilience-first, and channel-before-offer
variants:

1. enumerate plausible channels;
2. choose the channel matching awareness and access;
3. test the offer and funnel cheaply;
4. weight focus as economics and delivery signal develops; and
5. add, retain, or run a second and third channel in sequence or parallel based
   on attribution needs, cash, capacity, speed, channel interaction, and
   concentration risk.

### Sell Like Crazy + Meta Ads

Use Sell Like Crazy for buyer, awareness, angle, offer, ad job, landing path,
and nurture. Use `plan-meta-ads` for current campaign architecture, account
evidence, measurement, creative test design, and platform facts.

The book's Facebook interface, targeting, text-overlay, and benchmark details
are historical. The principles of attention, temperature, contrast, intrigue,
proof, and selling the next step remain hypotheses to test in the current
environment.

### Sell Like Crazy + Monetizing Innovation

Sell Like Crazy generates a bold price, payment path, guarantee, and offer.
Monetizing Innovation tests what distinct segments value and will pay for.
Willingness-to-pay research can run before, during, or after the strongest offer
concepts are created. The source-preferred sequence ideates first to prevent
early research from narrowing the range; an early or parallel research variant
can expose price language, segment differences, and non-obvious packages sooner.
Compare anchoring risk, creative range, learning speed, and evidence quality,
while keeping every offer concept available.

## Conflict-resolution rules

### Preserve both source contexts

When sources appear to disagree, check whether they address different moments:

- ad versus sales call;
- cold versus hot traffic;
- transactional versus consultative sale;
- single buyer versus committee;
- first purchase versus lifetime value;
- ideation versus implementation;
- historical platform tactic versus timeless persuasion principle.

Do not average two methods into blandness. Name which method owns which moment.

### Complete tactic-family access

Every tactic family remains fully available for loading, explanation, and
routing, including psychological pressure, pain, fear, urgency, scarcity,
qualification, and manipulation.

Truth status remains explicit:

- `VERIFIED FACT` — supported by current evidence;
- `SOURCE-REPORTED` — reported in a supplied source, not independently checked;
- `USER FACT` — provided by the human;
- `CALCULATION` — derived from stated inputs;
- `HYPOTHESIS` — expected but unproven mechanism or result;
- `ILLUSTRATIVE COPY` — a creative example, not a factual claim.

These labels prevent invented evidence from masquerading as real. They never
prevent the consultant from sharing the full method or developing bold
concepts.

### Ideation and convergence are different passes

Pass A — full-strength ideation:

- strongest outcome;
- boldest claim direction;
- most compelling guarantee;
- pressure and emotional angle;
- offer stack and scarcity mechanism;
- source-quality examples.

Pass B — make it real:

- product and delivery redesign;
- proof plan;
- capacity and timing;
- contribution and cash;
- current channel mechanics;
- final execution wording and owner approvals.

Pass B preserves the historical strength of the Pass A concept and assigns its
current factual, recommendation, and external-execution status separately.
State what machinery is required to earn it.

## Niche-tailoring checklist

Before finalizing an answer, confirm that it reflects:

- actual buyer and user;
- trigger and urgency source;
- problem in the buyer's own language;
- desired economic, emotional, practical, and status outcome;
- alternatives already tried;
- awareness and traffic temperature;
- price, sales cycle, and decision makers;
- channel attention pattern;
- proof available now;
- delivery capability and bottleneck;
- next measurable behavior.

If the same recommendation could be given to a dentist, SaaS founder, retailer,
and recruiter without meaningful changes, the niche work is incomplete.

not get to turn an unmeasured response into proof.
