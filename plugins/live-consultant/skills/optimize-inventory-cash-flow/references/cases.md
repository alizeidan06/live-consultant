# Inventory and cash-flow cases

These are constructed examples unless explicitly identified as the shared
sanitized meeting case. They do not claim measured results.

## Weight-bound shipment with empty cube

A dense-product importer sees substantial empty space in a container. The
payload is already close to the route-legal weight limit. Filling visible cube
with more dense stock is not feasible. The correct decision compares lighter,
compatible filler SKUs only after demand commitment, incremental landed
contribution, handling, damage, customs, and cash timing are modeled. Empty
space alone is not a loss calculation.

## Fast seller with weak cash contribution

A SKU has the highest unit velocity but is discounted heavily, carries sales
commission and delivery cost, and is sold on long credit terms. A slower SKU
has a higher GMROI and collects deposits. The skill reports velocity,
contribution, and cash timing separately instead of declaring the unit-volume
leader the winner.

## Requested import that buyers abandon

Several buyers say they want a new product. The importer purchases a full lot;
buyers later decline. The next cycle moves demand through samples, qualified
quotes, written intent, purchase orders where applicable, deposits, paid
orders, and repeats. Verbal interest remains useful discovery evidence, but it
does not receive the cash-risk weight of a deposit.

## Channel migration without self-cannibalization

An established dealer channel provides most current cash while a direct buyer
segment appears attractive. The business protects dealer service, runs a
bounded direct pilot with distinct attribution and economics, proves
incremental contribution and repeat behavior, and only then changes channel
allocation. A segment hypothesis does not justify abandoning working cash flow.
