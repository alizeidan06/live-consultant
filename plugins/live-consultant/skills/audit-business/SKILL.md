# Audit Business

At the start of every invocation, read and apply the mandatory
[Live Consultant communication voice](../founder-business-consultant/references/communication-voice.md).
Use it for every user-facing answer and customer-facing artifact. Simplify the
language, never the reasoning, stakes, specificity, or commercial force.

Before selecting research, examples, or tactics, also read and apply the
[niche intelligence protocol](../founder-business-consultant/references/niche-intelligence-protocol.md).
Infer the niche from available context first. Ask only questions whose answers
could change the research route or recommendation; otherwise proceed with a
labeled reversible assumption.

Audit the system the user actually has, not an imagined best-practice business.
Read the owner's standard and current artifacts first. An audit request permits
inspection and recommendations, not unrequested external changes.

## Establish scope and access

Record:

- business stage, buyer, offer, channel, geography, and time period;
- requested audit surfaces and explicit exclusions;
- artifacts actually available: P&L, CRM, analytics, ads export, page, calls,
  fulfillment data, support logs, contracts, or screenshots;
- freshness, completeness, attribution gaps, and inaccessible systems;
- the metric of record and decision the audit must support.

Do not score an unobserved surface. Mark it `NOT ASSESSED`.

## Build the evidence baseline

Read [audit-scorecard.md](references/audit-scorecard.md). Establish current
values before benchmarks:

1. demand and buyer evidence;
2. problem, product, and value realization;
3. positioning, offer, price, and terms;
4. contribution economics and cash timing;
5. acquisition and channel fit;
6. sales and conversion path;
7. fulfillment, quality, retention, refunds, and capacity;
8. website, funnel, tracking, and message match;
9. governance, compliance, privacy, security, and permissions;
10. team, founder time, processes, and operational resilience.

Use `verified`, `reported`, `inferred`, or `unknown` for each finding. Never
convert missing data into a negative fact.

## Find the binding constraint

1. Map symptom → candidate causes → discriminating evidence.
2. Reproduce important calculations and reconcile source totals.
3. Separate demand, offer, creative, targeting, page, sales, delivery, and
   retention problems. A downstream symptom may have an upstream cause.
4. Attempt to refute the leading diagnosis with the strongest competing cause.
5. Identify the one constraint that currently limits the desired outcome. If
   several remain plausible, define the smallest test that separates them.
6. Sweep the error class: one broken event implies checking every critical
   event; one unsupported claim implies a full claims sweep.

## Report findings

Use [finding-schema.md](references/finding-schema.md). Rank:

- `BLOCKING`: prevents a safe or valid decision, launch, or scale action;
- `MATERIAL`: meaningfully changes economics, customer outcome, or confidence;
- `POLISH`: improves quality after the constraint is solved;
- `CLEAN`: explicitly checked and not found defective.

End with `PASS`, `REVISE`, `REBUILD`, `PARK`, or `INSUFFICIENT EVIDENCE`.
Provide one highest-leverage fix, the smallest proof step, success/stop
thresholds, owner approvals, and the verification method. Do not hand back a
flat list of generic recommendations.
